# 📊 Marketing Funnel & Conversion Performance Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing a marketing campaign dataset to understand customer conversion behavior and identify opportunities to improve conversion rates.

The objective is to evaluate how different customer segments respond to marketing efforts and to uncover patterns that can help businesses optimize their campaigns and increase revenue.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

* Analyze customer conversion behavior
* Identify high and low performing segments
* Evaluate the impact of marketing efforts (campaign calls)
* Provide actionable business recommendations

---

## 🛠️ Tools Used

* Power BI – Data visualization and dashboard creation
* Excel/CSV – Data source

---

## 📁 Dataset

* Bank Marketing Dataset (UCI Repository)
* Contains customer demographics, campaign details, and conversion outcomes

---

## 📊 Key Metrics

* Total Customers
* Total Conversions
* Conversion Rate (%)

---

## 📈 Dashboard Insights

### 🔹 Conversion by Job

* Certain job roles such as students and retired individuals show higher conversion rates
* Blue-collar segments show relatively lower conversions

### 🔹 Conversion by Education

* Customers with higher education (tertiary) tend to convert more
* Basic education segments have lower engagement

### 🔹 Conversion by Age Group

* Younger (18–25) and older (50+) segments show better conversion performance
* Middle age groups show moderate conversion

### 🔹 Conversion vs Campaign Calls

* Conversion rate decreases as the number of calls increases
* Indicates over-contacting negatively impacts customer response

---

## 🧠 Key Insights

* Targeted segments perform significantly better than the overall average
* Over-calling leads to reduced conversion efficiency
* Customer demographics strongly influence campaign success

---

## 💡 Recommendations

* Focus marketing efforts on high-converting segments (students, retirees, educated users)
* Limit the number of campaign calls to avoid customer fatigue
* Implement personalized marketing strategies
* Redesign approach for low-performing segments
